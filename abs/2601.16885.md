# GPA-VGGT: Adapting VGGT to Large Scale Localization by Self-Supervised Learning with Geometry and Physics Aware Loss

**arXiv:** [2601.16885](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.16885)

## Abstract

Transformer-based visual geometry models have shown strong potential for camera pose estimation and 3D scene understanding from video streams. Among them, Visual Geometry Grounded Transformer (VGGT) provides a powerful framework for joint camera and geometry modeling, but its training typically depends on large amounts of ground-truth supervision, which limits its applicability to unlabeled and unseen large-scale environments. This paper presents a self-supervised framework to adapt VGGT for large-scale visual localization from unlabeled driving videos. Instead of relying on conventional pairwise supervision, the method extends self-supervision to sequence-wise geometric constraints that exploit richer temporal and multi-view consistency within long video streams. Multiple source frames are sampled in each sequence and geometrically projected onto different target frames to reinforce cross-view feature alignment and long-range spatial consistency. A joint optimization objective couples photometric consistency with geometric constraints, enabling the model to learn physically grounded camera motion and scene structure without hard labels. Experiments on challenging large-scale outdoor benchmarks demonstrate consistent improvements in long-range localization accuracy and trajectory stability in dynamic and adverse environments.

## 摘要

基于 Transformer 的视觉几何模型在从视频流进行相机位姿估计和三维场景理解方面展现出巨大潜力。其中，VGGT 为联合相机与几何建模提供了强大框架，但其训练通常依赖大量真值监督，限制了在未标注大规模环境中的应用。本文提出一种自监督框架，利用未标注驾驶视频将 VGGT 适配到大规模视觉定位。该方法将自监督扩展到序列级几何约束，充分利用长视频流中更丰富的时序和多视角一致性。通过将光度一致性与几何约束耦合的联合优化目标，使模型无需硬标签即可学习物理感知的相机运动和场景结构。在大规模户外基准测试上，该方法在动态和恶劣环境下持续提升了远程定位精度和轨迹稳定性。
