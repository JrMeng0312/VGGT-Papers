### VGGT-World: Transforming VGGT into an Autoregressive Geometry World Model

World models that forecast scene evolution by generating future video frames devote the bulk of their capacity to photometric details, yet the resulting predictions often remain geometrically inconsistent. We present VGGT-World, a geometry world model that side-steps video generation entirely and instead forecasts the temporal evolution of frozen geometry-foundation-model (GFM) features. Concretely, we repurpose the latent tokens of a frozen VGGT as the world state and train a lightweight temporal flow transformer to autoregressively predict their future trajectory. Two technical challenges arise in this high-dimensional feature space: (i) standard velocity-prediction flow matching collapses, and (ii) autoregressive rollout suffers from compounding exposure bias. We address the first with a clean-target parameterization that yields a substantially higher signal-to-noise ratio, and the second with a two-stage latent flow-forcing curriculum that progressively conditions the model on its own partially denoised rollouts. Experiments on KITTI, Cityscapes, and TartanAir demonstrate that VGGT-World significantly outperforms the strongest baselines in depth forecasting while running 3.6-5 times faster with only 0.43B trainable parameters.

通过生成未来视频帧来预测场景演变的世界模型将大量能力投入到光度细节上，但生成的预测往往在几何上不一致。我们提出 VGGT-World，一个完全绕过视频生成的几何世界模型，转而预测冻结的几何基础模型（GFM）特征的时间演变。具体而言，我们将冻结 VGGT 的潜在令牌作为世界状态，训练一个轻量级时间流 Transformer 自回归预测其未来轨迹。在这个高维（d=1024）特征空间中出现了两个技术挑战：(i) 标准速度预测流匹配会崩溃，(ii) 自回归推演会累积曝光偏差。我们通过净目标参数化解决第一个问题以获得更高的信噪比，通过两阶段潜在流强制课程逐步让模型在自身部分去噪的推演结果上进行条件化来解决第二个问题。在 KITTI、Cityscapes 和 TartanAir 上的实验表明，VGGT-World 在深度预测方面显著优于最强基线，同时速度快 3.6-5 倍，仅需 0.43B 可训练参数。
